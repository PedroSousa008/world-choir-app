On one day, at one exact second, voices from every corner of the planet will rise together.

We chose "Imagine" by John Lennon because it carries the message the world needs most — hope, unity, and the belief that even strangers can share the same heartbeat for a moment.

"You may say I'm a dreamer"

"But I'm not the only one"

"I hope someday you'll join us"

"And the world will be as one."

When you sing these words, you're not singing alone. Someone in Tokyo, someone in São Paulo, someone in New York, someone in Lisbon — they are all singing with you, at the same second, sharing the same message.

For a few minutes, the world pauses... and becomes a choir.

A reminder that there are no borders in music.

A reminder that peace can start with something as small as your voice.

Join us.

Sing with us.

And let the world be as one.

One Countdown. One Voice. One World.

In an era defined by disconnection and distraction, the World Choir App proposes a singular moment of unity: a countdown that brings the world together — not online, but out into the streets, onto balconies, across parks, windows, and plazas — to sing. Inspired by a stunning moment from a recent Got Talent audition, where an entire crowd joined in song within seconds, this concept translates that collective emotion into a global, real-world phenomenon.

This isn’t an app for content creation or audio recording — it is a global trigger for participation. A moment shared. A memory made. A chorus echoed across borders.

Balconies breaking into harmony
City squares glowing with song
Neighbors united by a single countdown

Concept Summary

  • The Core: A beautifully simple mobile app with a global countdown.
  • The Experience: At zero, people everywhere sing the same song for 10–20 seconds — outdoors, together, filmed by themselves or those around them.
  • The Goal: Unity. Emotion. Presence. A once-in-a-lifetime shared experience, born in the real world, powered by digital simplicity.
  • The Vision: To create the first-ever real-time global singing event, coordinated entirely through a single countdown moment — no rehearsal, no instructions, just human connection.
